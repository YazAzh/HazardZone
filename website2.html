Definition and origin of the specific hazard

Tsunamis are giant waves/tidal waves

Tsunamis are giant waves/tidal waves that speed along as fast as jet planes. It may occur due to earthquakes, volcanic eruptions, or landslides. Tsunami waves do not dramatically increase in height, but as the waves get closer to the shore, the force of the waves gets stronger. Tsunamis originate from the motion of continental plates that is caused by earthquakes. The occurrence of forceful vertical motion causes the water column to fall or rise which forms tsunami waves.
